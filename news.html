Opening Day 03/30

It is the start to a brand new season. Will we be seeing a continuation of the dominant runs we have seen over the past few years? Or will new victors emerge?

Opening day gave us the first showing of many rookie stars. Anthony Volpe of the Yankees earned one walk and a stolen base in his first game of the show. We also got to witnessed Adley Rutschman go 5-5 in his opening day game. Hunter Greene of the Reds threw a pitch that hit 105mph.

We saw a noteable amount of teams get blanked today. Yankees/Giants (5-0), we saw Cole record 11 strikeouts, Webb with 12, Judge hit a HR at his first AB. Cubs/Brewers (4-0), Swanson recorded three hits, Yelich walked twice, Turang recorded a hit in his first MLB game. Rays/Tigers (4-0), Franco and Siri homered, Austin Meadows went 3-4, and Riley Greene got a hit.

Rutschman and the Orioles had an exciting day against the Red Sox (10-9). Going 5-5, Rutschman led Baltimore, but he wasn't the only bat, as a team they recorded 15 hits, nine walks, and five stolen bases. The Braves beat the Nationals (7-2) in their debut as well. Travis d'Arnaud of the Braves recorded 4 hits on 5 AB. CJ Abrams was a disappointment going 0-4 and 3 errors.

Twins/Royals (2-0) ended up being an interesting game where only 2 runs were scored. The Twins out-hit the Royals 10-2, yet the Twins were only able to capitalize with 2 runs. The Royals' bats just weren't there today with Witt, Melendez, and Pasquantino recording 0 hits together. It was a closer game than it should have been.

Mets/Marlins (5-3), a matchup between two great pitchers in Scherzer and Alcantara. The Mets were able to eke out the win with the help of a Brandon Nimmo double. Jazz Chisholm Jr. committed a costly throwing error in the sixth. Pirates/Reds (5-4), a great outing from Greene, 8 SO a 3 walks in 3 1/3 innings. The Pirates ended up on top thanks to Cruz's homer and Ji Hwan Bae's 2 hits.

Blue Jays/Cardinals (10-9) was another high scoring game on opening day with 19 total runs. The Jays' young talent really came to play today, Springer went 5-6, Bichette went 4-6, Guerrero went 2-4, Chapman went 3-5; the bats were there. Both teams' pitching got lit up with Manoah and Mikolas both finishing with a 13.5 ERA after pitching 3 1/3 innings each. The Cardinal's bats were there too with their entire starting rotation recording at least a hit.

White Sox/Astros (3-2) was an interesting game, for a while it seemed that the White Sox would never capitalize on their hits. Eventually, Vaughn, in his one hit of the game, drove in two runs to lead the Astros 3-1. Yordan Alvarez hit a bomb that went 442ft to bring it to the final 3-2. Him and Pena both had decent outings, but it was Anderson and Robert Jr. for the White Sox who lead the show, both batting 2-4 on the day. Pitching for the Sox was spectacular as Dylan Cease earned 10 K's in his opener. Valdez put up a great effort, while still allowing six hits, he struck out four, and was able to pitch out of trouble and stave off any runs.

Rockies/Padres (7-2), this game had an unexpected outcome. Rockies looked fantastic with their entire starting lineup recording at least a hit, with 17 combined. C.J. Cron was responsible for two HRs and drove 5 runs. The Padres are MLBs favorite to win the World Series, they did not play like it, Soto went 0-4, Machado got a hit that drove in a run. The bright spot for the Padres was Bogaerts in his opener with the club, batting 3-4 with 1 run.

Athletics/Angels (2-1), a low scoring effort. Ohtani recorded 10 strikeouts, 2 more than the entire Athletics rotation that night. No team really seemed to get their hitting in order last night, Trout, Rendon, Renfroe, all recorded 0 hits. Just the first game of the season, 161 games to go, but I cannot sit through another season of Ohtani keeping the scoring low only to be let down by his supporting cast of, what should be, really good players.

Dodgers/Diamondbacks (8-2) was a showcase of the Dodgers' talents and young core. Will Smith went 3-5 and drove in three runs, and James Outman hit a HR. Urias looked strong on the mound as well recording 6 SOs through 6 and allowing 2 runs. The Diamondbacks' lineup looked weak as they were only able to record 4 hits collectively.

Mariners/Guardians (3-0), Luis Castillo looked fantastic on the mound, breezing through batters with 6 SOs through 6, only allowing one hit. Great outing from Ty France who went 3-4 and drove in all 3 of the Mariners' runs. For the Guardians, Bieber looked great on the mound, allowing no runs through 6, he was able to get out of dangerous situations as he gave up 6 hits. The bats were weak on the Guardians' side as they were not able to capitalize on their 4 hits for the game.
