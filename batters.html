Top 50 Bats to Watch in 2023

1. Vladimir Guerrero Jr.

I think that this will be Vlad's breakout year. With 4 solid seasons under his belt, he is poised to continue his campaign. Composite projections for 2023 have him batting .290/.365/.531 a 21+% increase from his stats in 2022.

2. Paul Goldschmidt

Goldscmidt was able to add to his impressive resume his first MVP award last year. It will be hard to improve on such an impressive 2022 season, but it is unlikely that we will see Goldschmidt regress.

3. Yordan Alvarez

Yordan Alvarez is a beast. In 2022, he finished top-3 among qualified batters in wRC+, wRAA, wOBA, and xwOBA. He also batted .306/.405/.613. Composite projections have him regressing a bit, but should still finish as a top-10 batter.

4. Aaron Judge

133 runs scored. 62 home runs. A triple slash of .311/.425/.686. An OPS of 1.111. Judge had a histroic 2022 season. Projections are not up to par of his 2022 production, but did anyone expect Judge's 2022 season? You will hear his name in MVP discussions again in 2023.

5. Shohei Ohtani

I am ranking Shohei Ohtani at #5 solely on his batting ability. He has hit 30+ HRs in his last two seasons, and I expect this trend to continue. Coming off a WBC Championship win, I think that 2023 will be the year we see Shohei pitch in the post-season.

6. Freddie Freeman

Freeman put up great numbers in his first season with the Dodgers last year, with a 0.325 BA, 0.407 OBP, and placed third in xwOBA out of qualified hitters with .403. Composite projections have him having another top-10 season.
